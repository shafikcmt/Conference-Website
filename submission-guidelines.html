Submission Guidelines

Authors are invited to contribute to the conference by submitting original research articles that illustrate novel findings, significant projects, comprehensive surveys, or industrial experiences in the fields of Smart Technologies and Intelligent Computing. Submissions should highlight advancements, challenges, and opportunities within these domains.

Topics of interest include, but are not limited to:

  1. Smart Technologies
    • Smart Cities and Urban Computing
    • IoT and Edge Computing Applications
    • Cyber-Physical Systems
    • Smart Energy Systems and Smart Grids
    • Wearable and Ubiquitous Technologies

  2. Intelligent Computing
    • Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning Applications
    • Natural Language Processing and Chatbots
    • Computer Vision and Pattern Recognition
    • Deep Learning Architectures and Algorithms
    • Reinforcement Learning and Autonomous Systems

  3. Emerging Trends and Applications
    • Blockchain and Distributed Ledger Technologies
    • Quantum Computing Applications
    • Big Data Analytics and Cloud Computing
    • Cybersecurity and Privacy Solutions
    • Augmented and Virtual Reality

  4. Interdisciplinary and Industrial Innovations
    • Healthcare Technologies and AI in Medicine
    • Intelligent Transportation Systems
    • Industry 4.0 and Smart Manufacturing
    • Educational Technologies and E-Learning Systems
    • Environmental Monitoring and Sustainability

We encourage contributions that address real-world problems, propose innovative solutions, and discuss the implications of smart technologies and intelligent computing on society.

Interested authors are invited to submit full papers including results, figures and references. Papers will be accepted only by electronic submission through easy-chair link given below

Plagiarism Policy

The INCSTIC-25 Conference upholds the highest standards of academic integrity and regards plagiarism as a serious professional misconduct. All submitted papers will be rigorously screened for plagiarism using advanced detection tools. If plagiarism is identified, the paper will be immediately rejected, and the authors may be barred from future submissions.

We strongly encourage authors to ensure the originality of their work and appropriately cite all sources to avoid any ethical violations.

Conference Guidelines and Policies

The INCSTIC-25 Conference organizers maintain zero tolerance for plagiarism and non-compliance with the IEEE formatting template. Any violation of registration guidelines or instructions, at any stage of the publication process, will result in the cancellation of the paper's registration.

Authors must complete the e-copyright transfer process and present their paper at the conference. Non-compliance with these requirements will result in disqualification.

The corresponding author (as per the CMT system) is responsible for regularly checking their email, including the spam/junk folder, to stay updated on conference communications and provide any required information promptly.

The registration fee, once paid, is strictly non-refundable under any circumstances.
